# Chapter 4: Dates, Time, and Weather (Kalender, Uhrzeit und Wetter)

This chapter covers the essential vocabulary and structures for navigating the calendar, telling the time, organizing appointments, and discussing the weather.

---

## Days, Months, and Seasons

All days of the week, months, and seasons in German are **masculine** (they take the article **der**).

### 1. Days of the Week (Die Wochentage)
To say "on [Day]," use the prepositional contraction **am** (*ahm*), which combines *an* (on) + *dem* (the dative masculine article).

| German | Pronunciation | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **der Montag** | MON-tahk | Monday |
| **der Dienstag** | DEENS-tahk | Tuesday |
| **der Mittwoch** | MIT-vokh | Wednesday |
| **der Donnerstag** | DON-ners-tahk | Thursday |
| **der Freitag** | FRY-tahk | Friday |
| **der Samstag** | ZAHMS-tahk | Saturday *(Samstag is used everywhere; northern Germany sometimes uses Sonnabend)* |
| **der Sonntag** | ZON-tahk | Sunday |

* *Example*: **Am Montag arbeite ich.** (*Ahm MON-tahk ARR-by-tuh ikh.*) — On Monday, I work.

### 2. Months (Die Monate)
To say "in [Month]," use the contraction **im** (*im*), which combines *in* (in) + *dem* (the dative masculine article).

| German | Pronunciation | English | German | Pronunciation | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **der Januar** | YAH-noo-ar | January | **der Juli** | YOO-lee | July |
| **der Februar** | FAY-broo-ar | February | **der August** | ow-GOOST | August |
| **der März** | mehrtts | March | **der September** | zep-TEM-ber | September |
| **der April** | ah-PRIL | April | **der Oktober** | ok-TOH-ber | October |
| **der Mai** | my | May | **der November** | no-VEM-ber | November |
| **der Juni** | YOO-nee | June | **der Dezember** | dey-TSEM-ber | December |

* *Example*: **Im Juli ist es heiß.** (*Im YOO-lee ist es hyse.*) — In July, it is hot.

### 3. Seasons (Die Jahreszeiten)
Like months, use **im** to say "in [Season]".

* **der Frühling** (*FREW-ling*) — Spring
* **der Sommer** (*ZOM-mer*) — Summer
* **der Herbst** (*herpst*) — Autumn / Fall
* **der Winter** (*VIN-ter*) — Winter

## Telling the Time (Die Uhrzeit)

German has two systems for telling time: the **formal (24-hour)** system and the **informal (12-hour)** system.

```mermaid

    %% Informal time examples
    t1 : "Informal: viertel nach"
    t2 : "Informal: halb"
    t3 : "Informal: viertel vor"
```

### 1. Formal Time (Official, 24-Hour)
Used for radio, TV, timetables, and official appointments. It is extremely simple:
* **Formula**: `[Hours] + Uhr + [Minutes]`

* **08:15** -> acht Uhr fünfzehn (*akht oor fwenf-tsayn*)
* **14:30** -> vierzehn Uhr dreißig (*FEER-tsayn oor DRY-sikh*)
* **19:45** -> neunzehn Uhr fünfundvierzig (*NOYN-tsayn oor fwenf-oont-FEER-sikh*)

### 2. Informal Time (Conversational, 12-Hour)
Used in casual speech. It uses relative terms like **vor** (before/to), **nach** (after/past), and **halb** (half).

> [!WARNING]
> **The "Halb" Rule**: In German, **halb** means "halfway TO the next hour", not "half past".
> * **halb vier** (half four) does NOT mean 4:30. It means **3:30** (halfway to 4).
> * **halb acht** (half eight) means **7:30**.

* **nach** = past / after
* **vor** = to / before
* **viertel nach** = quarter past
* **viertel vor** = quarter to

#### Examples:
* **08:05** -> fünf nach acht (*fwenf nahkh akht*)
* **08:15** -> viertel nach acht (*FEER-tel nahkh akht*)
* **08:25** -> fünf vor halb neun (*fwenf for hahlp noyn*) — *5 minutes before half 9*
* **08:30** -> halb neun (*hahlp noyn*) — *halfway to 9*
* **08:35** -> fünf nach halb neun (*fwenf nahkh hahlp noyn*) — *5 minutes past half 9*
* **08:45** -> viertel vor neun (*FEER-tel for noyn*)
* **08:50** -> zehn vor neun (*tsayn for noyn*)

To ask for the time:
* **Wie spät ist es?** (*Vee shpayt ist es?*) — How late is it?
* **Wie viel Uhr ist es?** (*Vee feel oor ist es?*) — What time is it?

## Date Formats and Appointments

### Writing and Saying the Date
In German, dates are written in the order **Day. Month. Year** (with periods).
* **29.06.2026** is spoken using ordinal numbers:
  * **der neunundzwanzigste Juni zweitausendsechsundzwanzig**

To say "on the [Date]", use **am** followed by the ordinal ending in **-ten**:
* **am neunundzwanzigsten Juni** (*ahm NOYN-oont-tsvan-tsikh-sten YOO-nee*) — on the 29th of June.

### Making an Appointment (Termin vereinbaren)
* **Wann haben Sie Zeit?** (*Vann HAH-ben zee tsyt?*) — When do you have time? (Formal)
* **Hast du am Freitag Zeit?** (*Hahst doo ahm FRY-tahk tsyt?*) — Do you have time on Friday? (Informal)
* **Passt es dir um drei Uhr?** (*Pahst es deer oom dry oor?*) — Does three o'clock suit you?

## Weather Vocabulary (Das Wetter)

To describe the weather, German uses the pronoun **es** (it) + verb, or **es ist** (it is) + adjective.

| German | Pronunciation | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Es ist kalt.** | es ist kahlt | It is cold. |
| **Es ist heiß.** | es ist hyse | It is hot. |
| **Es regnet.** | es REKH-net | It is raining. |
| **Es schneit.** | es shnyte | It is snowing. |
| **Die Sonne scheint.** | dee ZON-uh shynt | The sun is shining. |
| **Es ist windig.** | es ist VIN-dikh | It is windy. |
| **Es ist bewölkt.** | es ist buh-VUELKT | It is cloudy. |
| **Das Wetter ist schön.** | dahs VET-ter ist shoen | The weather is beautiful. |
